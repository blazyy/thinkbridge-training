Pizza Margherita Recipe

The secret to a great pizza Margherita is to use the best ingredients you can find—and to approach them with restraint. (Just because a little cheese is good doesn't mean a lot will be better!) This slightly wet dough, in conjunction with a hot pizza stone, produces a crisp yet chewy crust, the perfect canvas for bright homemade tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and verdant basil leaves.

Serves: 2

Active Cook Time: 35 min

Total Cook Time: 1 hr 45 min

Ingredients

For Dough

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 220 grams all-purpose flour
  • 180 ml warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon olive oil

For Topping

  • 400 grams of tomato puree
  • 2 large garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 basil leaves
  • ¼ teaspoon sugar
  • 170gm shredded mozzarella
  • 4 basil leaves

Equipment

  • Pizza stone
  • Large bowl
  • Blender
  • Saucepan
  • Parchment paper

Preparation

Step 1) Make Dough

  1. Stir together yeast, 1 tablespoon flour, and 60 ml warm water in a large bowl and let stand until surface appears creamy, about 5 minutes. (If mixture doesn't appear creamy, discard and start over with new yeast.)
  2. Add 170 gm flour, remaining 120 ml water, salt, and oil and stir until smooth. Stir in enough flour (about 30g) for dough to begin to pull away from side of bowl. (Dough will be slightly wet.)
  3. Knead on a floured surface, lightly reflouring when dough becomes too sticky, until smooth, soft, and elastic, about 8 minutes. Form into a ball, put in a bowl, and dust with flour. Cover with plastic wrap or a kitchen towel (not terry cloth) and let rise in a draft-free place at warm room temperature until doubled, about 1 1/4 hours.

Step 2) Make tomato sauce while dough rises

  1. Pulse tomatoes with juice in a blender briefly to make a chunky purée.
  2. Cook garlic in oil in a small heavy saucepan over medium-low heat until fragrant and pale golden, about 2 minutes. Add tomato purée, basil, sugar, and 1/8 teaspoon salt and simmer, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until thickened and reduced to about half its original volume, about 40 minutes. Season with salt and cool.

Step 3) Heat pizza stone while dough rises

  1. At least 45 minutes before baking pizza, put stone on oven rack in lower third of electric oven (or on floor of gas oven) and preheat oven to 260°C.

Step 4) Shape Dough

  1. Do not punch down. Dust dough with flour, then transfer to a parchment-lined pizza peel or large baking sheet. Pat out dough evenly with your fingers and stretch into a 14-inch round, reflouring fingers if necessary.

Step 5) Assemble Pizza

  1. Spread sauce over dough, leaving a 1-inch border (there may be some sauce left over). Arrange cheese on top, leaving a 2- to 3-inch border.
  2. Slide pizza on parchment onto pizza stone. Bake until dough is crisp and browned and cheese is golden and bubbling in spots, 13 to 16 minutes. Using peel or baking sheet, transfer pizza to a cutting board. Cool 5 minutes. Sprinkle with some basil leaves before slicing.

Cooks' Notes

  • Dough can be allowed to rise slowly in the refrigerator (instead of in a warm place) for 1 day. Bring to room temperature before shaping.
  • Tomato sauce can be made 5 days ahead and chilled.
